<p><p>The Glenoid Fossa Prosthesis market is primarily divided into cemented and cementless types. Cemented prostheses rely on polymethylmethacrylate (PMMA) to bond the implant firmly within the bone, providing immediate stability. In contrast, cementless prostheses utilize a rough surface or porous materials that encourage bone ingrowth for long-term fixation, allowing for better biological integration over time. Both types cater to different patient needs and surgical considerations, influencing their adoption in shoulder arthroplasty.</p></p>
