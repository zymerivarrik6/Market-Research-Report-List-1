<p><p>The Idiopathic Intracranial Hypertension (IIH) treatment market comprises various medications aimed at managing elevated intracranial pressure. Acetazolamide is the first-line treatment, functioning as a carbonic anhydrase inhibitor to reduce cerebrospinal fluid production. Methazolamide, a similar drug, is used when acetazolamide is ineffective or not tolerated. Furosemide, a diuretic, helps reduce fluid retention. Topiramate, an anticonvulsant, can alleviate headaches and has weight loss benefits. Other treatments may include surgical interventions or alternative therapies, catering to diverse patient needs.</p></p>
