<p><p>The DNA sequencing equipment and service market encompasses various technologies categorized into first, second, third, and fourth generation sequencing. First-generation sequencing relies on Sanger methods, ideal for accurate, short sequences. Second-generation sequencing utilizes high-throughput techniques for large-scale projects, enhancing speed and reducing costs. Third-generation sequencing focuses on real-time analysis of long DNA strands, enabling more comprehensive genomic studies. Fourth-generation sequencing seeks to further improve accuracy and efficiency, incorporating advancements like nanopore technology for rapid sequencing directly from living cells.</p></p>
