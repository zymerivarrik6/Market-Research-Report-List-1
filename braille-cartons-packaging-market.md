<p><p>Braille cartons packaging primarily utilizes two printing techniques: embossing and screen printing. Embossing creates raised text or symbols on the carton, allowing visually impaired individuals to easily identify information through touch. This method offers durability and a tactile experience. On the other hand, screen printing involves applying ink to the surface, enabling vibrant designs and detailed braille text. Both techniques serve the essential purpose of enhancing accessibility, ensuring that packaging is informative and user-friendly for all consumers.</p></p>
