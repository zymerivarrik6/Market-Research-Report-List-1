<p><p>Weather stations are categorized into wired and wireless types based on their connectivity. Wired weather stations use physical cables to connect sensors to a central unit, ensuring stable data transmission, often requiring installation expertise. In contrast, wireless weather stations utilize radio signals or Wi-Fi for communication, offering greater flexibility and easier installation. Wireless models tend to be more user-friendly, allowing for remote monitoring via smartphones or computers, making them increasingly popular among both amateur and professional meteorologists.</p></p>
