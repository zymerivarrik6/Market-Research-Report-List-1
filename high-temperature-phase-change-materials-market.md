<p><p>The high-temperature phase change materials (PCMs) market consists of three primary types: organic, inorganic, and bio-based materials. Organic PCMs, such as fatty acids and paraffins, offer stability and low toxicity but may have lower thermal conductivity. Inorganic PCMs, including salt hydrates and metallic alloys, exhibit higher thermal storage capacities but can face issues like corrosion. Bio-based PCMs derive from renewable sources, providing sustainability and reduced environmental impact, making them increasingly attractive in eco-friendly applications.</p></p>
